Google Workspace Password Reset Policy

Purpose:
To provide employees with clear instructions for resetting their corporate Google Workspace password, ensuring secure account recovery and minimal disruption to productivity.

	1	Scope: This policy applies to all employees, contractors, and consultants using a company-issued Google Workspace account (firstname.lastname@company.com).
	2	Self-Service Password Reset
		- Go to https://accounts.google.com/signin/recovery and enter your corporate email address.
		- Click "Forgot password?" and verify your identity using one of: recovery phone number, recovery email, security question, or a prompt on a trusted device.
		- Once verified, create a new password following the requirements in section 3.
		- Click "Change password" to confirm.
	3	Password Requirements
		- Minimum 12 characters.
		- Must include at least one uppercase letter, one lowercase letter, one number, and one special character.
		- Cannot reuse any of your last 10 passwords.
		- Cannot contain your name, username, or common dictionary words.
		- Expires every 90 days; a reminder is sent 14 days before expiration.
	4	Account Lockout
		- Account locks after 5 consecutive failed login attempts.
		- Lockout duration: 30 minutes (auto-unlock) or contact IT for immediate unlock.
		- If unauthorized access is suspected, report immediately to security@company.com.
	5	If Self-Service Recovery Is Unavailable
		- Contact the IT Helpdesk via phone: +1-800-123-4567 or submit a request at https://helpdesk.company.com.
		- You will be asked to verify your identity by providing your employee ID, manager's name, and department.
		- IT will issue a temporary password within 1 business hour.
		- You must change the temporary password immediately upon first login.
	6	After Resetting Your Password
		- Update your password on all devices: laptop/desktop (re-sign into Chrome), mobile phone (Gmail, Calendar, Drive apps), tablets, applications using Google SSO, and VPN client if applicable.
		- Sign out and back in on each device to clear cached credentials.
	7	Setting Up Recovery Options
		- Sign in to https://myaccount.google.com and go to Security > Ways we can verify it's you.
		- Add or update your recovery phone number and recovery email (use a personal email you control).
		- Set up 2-Step Verification with a backup method (security key, authenticator app, or backup codes).
	8	Common Issues
		- "Password recently used": You cannot reuse your last 10 passwords. Choose a new one.
		- "Account suspended": Contact IT immediately — your account may have been flagged by security.
		- "Cannot access recovery options": Visit IT helpdesk in person with a valid photo ID for manual verification.
		- "Password changed but apps still not working": Sign out and back in on each device; clear cached credentials if needed.

Effective Date: January 15th, 2025
Approved by: IT Governance Committee
