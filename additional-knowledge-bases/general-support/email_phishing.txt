Corporate Email Phishing Detection and Reporting

Purpose:
To help employees identify, avoid, and report phishing emails targeting corporate accounts.

	1	Scope: This policy applies to all employees, contractors, and consultants using company-issued email accounts (Microsoft 365 / Outlook).
	2	Common Signs of a Phishing Email
		- Sender address does not match the display name (e.g., "IT Support" but from random-string@external-domain.com).
		- Urgent or threatening language ("Your account will be suspended in 24 hours").
		- Unexpected attachments, especially .zip, .exe, .docm, or password-protected files.
		- Links that do not match the displayed text (hover to check the actual URL before clicking).
		- Requests for credentials, personal information, or payment outside of normal processes.
		- Generic greetings ("Dear User") instead of your name.
		- Poor grammar, misspellings, or unusual formatting inconsistent with the claimed sender.
		- "Reply-to" address differs from the "From" address.
	3	What To Do If You Suspect a Phishing Email
		- Do NOT click any links or open any attachments.
		- Do NOT reply to the email or forward it to colleagues.
		- Step 1: Click the "Report Phishing" button in Outlook (located in the ribbon or right-click menu).
		- Step 2: If the "Report Phishing" button is unavailable, forward the email as an attachment to phishing@company.com.
		- Step 3: Delete the email from your inbox after reporting.
		- The security team reviews all submissions and will notify you if further action is needed.
	4	What To Do If You Clicked a Phishing Link or Entered Credentials
		- Step 1: Immediately reset your password at https://accounts.google.com/signin/recovery or https://passwordreset.company.com.
		- Step 2: Report the incident to security@company.com with the subject line "Phishing - Credentials Compromised."
		- Step 3: Note the time you clicked and any information you entered.
		- Step 4: Do NOT delete the email — security will need it for investigation.
		- Step 5: The security team will assess your account for unauthorized access and may temporarily lock it as a precaution.
		- Step 6: Enable or verify 2-Step Verification on your account if not already active.
	5	What To Do If You Opened a Suspicious Attachment
		- Step 1: Disconnect from Wi-Fi or unplug the ethernet cable immediately.
		- Step 2: Do NOT restart or shut down the computer (this may destroy forensic evidence).
		- Step 3: Call the IT Helpdesk at +1-800-555-HELP (4357) and report what was opened.
		- Step 4: IT will remotely assess and may quarantine your device.
		- Step 5: Do not use the device until cleared by IT.
	6	Common Phishing Scenarios in Corporate Environments
		- Fake password reset emails impersonating IT or Google/Microsoft.
		- Invoices or purchase orders from unknown vendors requesting immediate payment.
		- "Shared document" notifications with links to credential harvesting pages.
		- Messages impersonating executives requesting urgent wire transfers or gift card purchases (CEO fraud / business email compromise).
		- Calendar invites from unknown senders containing malicious links.
		- Fake delivery notifications (FedEx, UPS, DHL) with tracking links.
	7	How To Verify a Suspicious Email
		- Hover over links to inspect the actual URL — legitimate company links use company.com domains.
		- Check the sender's full email address (click or expand the "From" field), not just the display name.
		- Contact the supposed sender through a separate channel (phone, Teams, or a new email) to confirm they sent it.
		- If the email claims to be from IT or security, verify by calling the IT Helpdesk directly.
		- Never use contact information provided in the suspicious email itself.
	8	Phishing Simulation Program
		- The company conducts periodic phishing simulations to test awareness.
		- If you click a simulated phish, you will be redirected to a training page — this is not a penalty.
		- Repeated failures on simulations may require completion of additional security awareness training.
		- Reporting a simulation correctly counts positively toward department security metrics.
	9	Reporting Channels Summary
		- Report Phishing button in Outlook: preferred method for suspected phishing emails.
		- phishing@company.com: forward as attachment if the button is unavailable.
		- security@company.com: for compromised credentials, opened attachments, or active security incidents.
		- IT Helpdesk (+1-800-555-HELP): for urgent situations requiring immediate device isolation.
